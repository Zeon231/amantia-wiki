# Currency & Wealth

> [!info] In Amantia
> Wealth appears in coins, gemstones, trade goods, art objects, animals, and property. Peasants barter in goods; nobles trade in legal rights or gold bars by the pound. Only merchants, adventurers, and professionals commonly deal in coins.

---

## Coins

| Coin | Abbreviation | Common Use |
|---|---|---|
| Copper piece | cp | Laborers, beggars — a candle, a torch, chalk |
| Silver piece | sp | Commoners — half a day's labor, lamp oil, a poor inn |
| Gold piece | gp | Standard unit of wealth — a bedroll, 50 ft rope, a goat |
| Electrum piece | ep | Rare; from fallen empires — sometimes met with suspicion |
| Platinum piece | pp | Rare; extreme wealth |

*A standard coin weighs ~⅓ oz. — 50 coins = 1 pound.*

## Selling Treasure

| Category | Sale Value |
|---|---|
| Arms, Armor & Equipment | ½ cost (if undamaged) |
| Magic Items | Effectively priceless — few buyers, no fixed price |
| Gems, Jewelry & Art Objects | Full value |
| Trade Goods | Full value |

> [!tip] Magic Items
> Finding a buyer for a potion or scroll is doable. Other magic items are beyond all but the wealthiest nobles. Treat magic as worth far more than gold.
