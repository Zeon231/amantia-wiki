# The Amantian Calendar

## Year Structure

The Amantian year has **365 days**, divided into four seasons called **Turns**, each 90 days long, separated by holiday festivals.

| Turn / Holiday | Days |
|----------------|------|
| New Year's Festival | 1 |
| **Silver Turn** (Winter) | 90 |
| Spring Festival | 1 |
| **Emerald Turn** (Spring) | 90 |
| Summer Festival | 1 |
| **Ruby Turn** (Summer) | 90 |
| Harvest Festival | 1 |
| **Gold Turn** (Autumn) | 90 |

The five festival days use **Arcavéan deity names** by convention; other regions substitute their own culturally relevant gods while preserving the spirit of each observance.

---

## Structure of a Turn

Each Turn is divided into **nine 10-day spans**. Each span has 7 workdays and 3 days of rest. The Arcavéan standard names:

| Day | Named For | Type |
|-----|-----------|------|
| **Virelysse** | Dawn | **Rest** |
| **Estivaldras** | Sun | Work |
| **Selmyra** | Moon | Work |
| **Asterday** | Stars | Work |
| **Umbraeia** | Shadows | **Rest** (household bonds & devotion) |
| **Durmanitus** | Water | Work |
| **Agronara** | Earth | Work |
| **Bellignatus** | Fire | Work |
| **Auranday** | Air | Work |
| **Aestara** | Dusk | **Rest** |

> [!note] Regional Variation
> Other cultures preserve the 10-day structure and 7/3 work-rest split but use their own deity names and may shift which days are rest days.

There are **24 hours in a civil day**.

---

## Lunar Cycle

Amantia has **one moon**. Its phases within a 30-day cycle:
- Day 1 — Full
- Day 5 — Three-quarter (waning)
- Day 10 — One-third (waning)
- Day 15 — Dark (new moon)
- Day 20 — One-third (waxing)
- Day 25 — Three-quarter (waxing)
- Day 30 — Full again

The moon governs tides at roughly **12-hour intervals**. Intercalary festival days also serve as practical calibrations keeping the civil count aligned with lunar and seasonal reality.

---

## Dating Convention

Years are counted as **BA** (Before the Arcadian Age) or **AR** (After Reckoning). The current year in Arcavéan archives is **1681 AR** at the time of the Annals of Amantia.
