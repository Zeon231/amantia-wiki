# Winter Court Faerie

![[04 - Species/Species Images/winter-court-faerie.jpg]]

Full Fae — beings of the Feywild's Unseelie Court. Winter Court Faerie are tied to cold, silence, endings, and preservation. Precise, deliberate, and often calculating, they understand the necessity of death and dormancy in the natural cycle — and they are utterly without sentiment about it. They are not cruel by nature; they are simply honest about what the world is.

> [!info] Racial Traits
> **Creature Type:** Fae (Full Fae)
> **Fey Ancestry:** Advantage on saves vs. charm; immune to magical sleep.
> **Darkvision:** 60 ft.
> **Winter's Chill:** Resistance to cold damage. Surfaces you stand on develop a light frost; your breath fogs in cold air even when others' does not.
> **Winter Magic:** Cast *Fog Cloud* once per long rest; *Armor of Agathys* once per short rest. Charisma is the spellcasting ability.
> **Fey Step:** As a bonus action, teleport up to 30 ft to an unoccupied space you can see. Once per short rest.
> **Languages:** Fae, Common, Sylvan, and one additional language.

---

## Appearance

Skin carrying a cool pallor — whites, pale blues, and icy silvers. Frost forms where they linger too long. Hair in whites, silvers, and deep blues; eyes like ice over dark water. They move with deliberate economy — no wasted motion, no unnecessary expression. They are beautiful the way a glacier is beautiful: still, immense, and ancient.

---

## Culture & The Court Tension

Winter Court Faerie value patience, precision, and the long view. They make plans in decades. They remember slights from centuries past with the same clarity they remember what happened yesterday. Their apparent coldness is not emotional absence — they feel deeply — but they have learned that showing feeling is a vulnerability, and they do not give vulnerabilities away.

The **Summer and Winter Courts** exist in a state of ancient tension — rarely open war, but a slow, eternal rivalry played out through mortal proxies, political maneuvering, and the turning of seasons. A Winter Court Faerie meeting a Summer Court Faerie in the mortal world is never a coincidence.
