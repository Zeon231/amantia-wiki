# Harengon

![[04 - Species/Species Images/harengon.jpg]]

Rabbit-folk — humanoids with long ears, powerful legs built for explosive movement, and reflexes honed by a long evolutionary history of being prey. Harengon have transformed those instincts into a gift: they are perpetually alert, nearly impossible to surprise, and can turn a near-miss into an elegant side-step.

> [!info] Racial Traits
> **Creature Type:** Humanoid (Bestial)
> **Hare-Trigger:** Add your Proficiency bonus to Initiative rolls.
> **Leporine Senses:** Advantage on Wisdom (Perception) checks.
> **Lucky Footwork:** When you fail a Dexterity saving throw, use your reaction to add 1d4 to the result, potentially turning it into a success.
> **Rabbit Hop:** As a bonus action, jump a number of feet equal to 5× your Proficiency bonus without provoking opportunity attacks. Once per turn.

---

## Appearance

Long upright ears; soft fur in a wide range of colours (white, grey, brown, black, spotted); large dark eyes; powerful haunches. They tend toward lean builds with long legs relative to their torso. Always moving — a resting Harengon still twitches its nose.

---

## Found In

Rural and wild regions throughout Amantia. Not strongly tied to any single homeland; Harengon communities tend to be small and spread across pastoral and frontier areas.
