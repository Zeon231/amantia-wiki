## Tabaxi

Tabaxi names are descriptive phrases, not personal names in the conventional sense.

**Examples:** Cloud of the Mountaintop, Five Timber, Flask of Wine, Jade Shoe, Left-Handed Hummingbird, Seven Thundercloud, Skirt of Snakes, Smoking Mirror

*Tabaxi are often shortened to a single evocative word when among other species: Flask, Jade, Seven, Skirt, etc.*

## Naming Conventions by Human Ethnicity

| Ethnicity | Inspiration | Notes |
|-----------|-------------|-------|
| **Siórtharan** | Irish / Old Welsh | Gaelic-flavoured names; prefixes: Tir, Ae, Mae, Caer, Rí, Síor |
| **Iljarnskarn** | Old Norse / Germanic | Hard consonants; Norse-pattern names; -helm, -gar, -orn suffixes |
| **Malkûn** | Classical Arabic | Arabic name patterns; al- prefix common; û = long u |
| **Bakassari** | West African (Yoruba, Wolof) | Melodic, vowel-rich names |
| **Vanakuti** | Sanskrit / South Indian | Sanskrit-derived; longer compound names common |
| **Arcavéan** | Classical Greek | Greek roots; -os, -a, -e endings; see names above |
